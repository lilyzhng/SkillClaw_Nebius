## Problem Statements

### Statement 1: Edge Inference & Agents
Build applications with Nebius Token Factory's open LLMs that go beyond tool calls — across software, robotics, and devices. Build agentic pipelines, robotics control loops that can reason over context, retrieve knowledge, plan next steps, and take actions.

### Statement 2: Robotics Optimization, Training, & Tuning
Build a system that enables a robot to perceive its environment, reason about what's happening, and take meaningful physical actions. RL policies, visual+language→actions, predictive world models.

**Robotics resources:** Nebius AI Cloud Compute + simulation stack for motion gen, world models, RL/policy training. Selected teams get deployment time on **8–10 Unitree G1 Humanoid Robots** (subject to safety review).

## Judging Criteria (OFFICIAL)

1. **Impact Potential (20%)** — What is the project’s long-term potential for success? Will this project have a long-lasting impact on the industry, world, or any other areas? How useful and substantial is this project beyond the scope of the hackathon?

2. **Live Demo (45%)** — How well has the team implemented their core idea? Does it work well live? How is it presented?

3. **Creativity and Originality (35%)** — Has this concept been seen before? How unique or innovative is this project in its respective industry, field, or area? Does it tackle the problem statements in a unique way?

### Judging Process
1. First round: Assigned groups of judges, ~3 min pitch + 1-2 min Q&A
2. Final round: Top 6 teams demo on stage, ~3 min pitch + 2-3 min Q&A

### Submission
Submit via [cerebralvalley.ai form](https://cerebralvalley.ai/e/nebius-build-sf/hackathon/submit). Must include a **1-minute demo video** (YouTube/Loom/etc).
